# MRT North-South Line (Fase 2B)

The **MRT North-South Line (Fase 2B)** is a planned southern extension that will extend Jakarta's MRT network beyond the city limits toward Bogor.

## Project Information

**Line Code**: NS2B (Red)  
**Status**: Under Construction/Planning  
**Route**: Lebak Bulus - Bogor area  
**Expected Completion**: TBA

## Key Features

- Southern extension from current terminus
- Will extend into neighboring Depok/Bogor area
- Combination of elevated and underground sections
- Integration with existing rail infrastructure

## Planned Coverage

The extension will:

- Extend from Lebak Bulus southward
- Serve southern Jakarta suburbs
- Connect with Depok area
- Eventually reach toward Bogor corridor

## Strategic Vision

This extension aims to:

- Reduce pressure on heavily congested Bogor Line (KRL)
- Provide fast metro service to southern suburbs
- Support regional integration
- Enable transit-oriented development
- Offer alternative to car travel

## Regional Integration

Fase 2B represents:

- Cross-city boundary transit planning
- Jakarta-Bogor corridor development
- Regional transportation cooperation
- Long-term metropolitan strategy

## Expected Benefits

Once operational, will provide:

- Faster travel times to southern areas
- Reduced crowding on parallel KRL services
- New development opportunities
- Improved regional connectivity
- Better air quality through transit use

## Development Potential

The extension will likely:

- Spur residential development along the route
- Create new commercial centers
- Increase property values
- Support sustainable urban growth
- Enable compact, transit-oriented communities

## Coordination

The project requires:

- Multi-jurisdictional cooperation
- Integration with city planning
- Environmental consideration
- Stakeholder engagement
- Phased implementation

This ambitious extension will significantly expand Jakarta's rapid transit reach and serve as a model for regional metro development in Indonesia.
